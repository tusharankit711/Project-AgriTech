Corn

Timing

Sowing Time

Corn is typically sown during the summer season, around April to June in India, which coincides with the pre-monsoon and monsoon season.

Temperature Requirements

Minimum Temperature

Corn requires a minimum temperature of around 18-20°C for germination and early growth.

Optimum Temperature

The ideal temperature range for corn growth is 25-35°C during the growing season. Higher temperatures can enhance growth, but extreme heat during flowering can affect pollination and yield.

Humidity Requirements

Humidity

Corn cultivation prefers moderate humidity levels, similar to other cereal crops. High humidity can lead to fungal diseases, so moderate humidity is ideal.

Rainfall

Rainfall Requirements

Corn is a moderately water-intensive crop.

Rainfed Areas

Ideally, corn requires around 500-1000 mm of well-distributed rainfall during

Soil Requirements

Soil Type

Corn can grow in a wide range of soils but thrives best in well-drained loamy soils with good organic matter content.

Fertilizers

Common fertilizers used for corn include nitrogenous fertilizers (urea), phosphatic fertilizers (DAP), and potassic fertilizers (MOP). Nitrogen is particularly important for corn growth and yield.

Fertility

Corn requires good soil fertility, especially nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. Fertilizer application is based on soil testing and crop nutrient requirements.

Pesticides

Corn may require pesticides to control pests such as corn borers, aphids, and diseases like rust and leaf blight. Integrated pest management practices are employed to minimize pesticide use and maintain crop health.

Cultivation Practices

Land Preparation

Fields are prepared by plowing and harrowing to create a good seedbed.

Sowing

Corn seeds are sown directly into the soil at the appropriate spacing and depth.

Water Management

Adequate and timely irrigation is crucial, especially during critical growth stages like flowering and grain filling.

Weed Control

Effective weed management is essential to reduce competition for nutrients and water. Herbicides are commonly used for weed control in corn fields.

Harvesting

Timing

Corn is harvested after about 3-4 months from sowing, depending on the variety and growing conditions.

Method

Corn kernels are harvested and dried to a safe moisture content for storage or further processing.
